The Chronicle of Adellar

Born in Daggerford on Marpenoth the seventh to Lord Llwellyn Longhand, Daggerford's master of arms, and Lady Maedra Varna Shadowdusk, a member of one of Waterdeep's noble houses, Adellar's conception — much like the rest of his life — was shrouded in occult mystery.

His father, Llwellyn Longhand, was the bastard son of the Daggerford line, and so was forced to take his mother's maiden name to hide this fact. However, greatness was in his blood, filled with fire, ferocity, and an unwavering dedication to Daggerford. Llwellyn's father couldn't help but be proud of his secret heir. To both keep him close and reward his service, he was given the title of Master of Arms, a fitting title for the master spearman and one that would grant him status via being a Lord.

Adellar's mother, Maedra Varna Shadowdusk, is the niece of the patriarch of the Shadowdusk house. The Shadowdusks once boasted the title of being one of Waterdeep's top five wealthiest noble houses, their wealth supplied by explorations into the most dangerous parts of Faerûn, procuring riches from these excursions due to the house's magical might. But as ambition gave way to greed, the Shadowdusks brokered a deal with the mad chosen of Mystra, Alister Blackcloak.

Trading inside information and services to Blackcloak in exchange for safe passage into Undermountain, their goal was to delve into the Underdark, where the patriarch was told by his dreams that great power awaited the Shadowdusks. What they found forever put the noble house on a path that had them consorting with aberrations — even going so far as to replace living members of their family with the strange creatures that took their kin's forms, and produced progeny of their own.

Seeing that the house was getting sloppy in its ability to hide this vile relation with alien entities, a plan was hatched to move the house's operations to a less vigilant location, but close enough to take advantage of Waterdeep's infrastructure. The place of choice was Daggerford. Maedra was chosen as the one to marry Llwellyn to gain a foothold into the lands. The plan yielded even greater gains as Maedra got pregnant, right as the house officially turned fully to the occult upon finding a true patron in a being they called the Pale Lady.

Before the Shadowdusks could capitalize on their machinations, Waterdeep caught on to the foul acts and odd anomalies within their city, tracing them all back to House Shadowdusk. It was too difficult to hide the vast number of missing infants, and the bodies of them that turned up with strange growths and insectile eyes. A raid on the house was formed by the city.

In desperation, Maedra ran off with infant Adellar into the night, to the secret Shadowdusk compound in Undermountain, to perform a terrible ritual on the child that would transform him into a vessel for their patron. Llwellyn, with a small contingent of men from Daggerford, joined the Waterdhavian authorities on the raid, killing off the wretched, corrupted house and retrieving Adellar — though it was too late. The child was forever changed. Llwellyn, though he confronted Maedra, could not bring himself to slay her; instead he chose to take his boy home and left her and the rest of her fallen house to their fate.

Adellar was told of his mother — his father believing in the strength that truth provides. He was raised in Daggerford and showed a particular aptitude for conjuration magic. Believing that after not hearing anything about the Shadowdusks for a decade and a half the danger had passed, Llwellyn sent Adellar to Waterdeep to further his studies. It was during this time, at the age of 15, that the Time of Troubles struck.

Due to the ritual performed on him as a babe, as magic failed the world over, the freak occurrence caused a type of mental resonance that transplanted the mind of the teen to a place known as the Weird — a world of ivory madness outside known reality called the Far Realm, and more importantly, a world ruled by the Pale Lady. In reverse, the mind of the Pale Lady was transferred into the body of Adellar. Possessed by the entity, all memory and agency was lost to him. While time held no relevance to the boy — whose mind was broken and reshaped by the literal insanity of his environment and its denizens — the Pale Lady spent five years wearing the skin of a human, reconnecting with the remnants of House Shadowdusk and preparing for the maturation of what they called the Stellar Key.

Upon finally regaining his body, Adellar found that he had lost everything: his mind, to the Time of Troubles; his humanity, to the strange alterations wrought on his body; his beloved father, to assassins in a power play for Daggerford's rulership; his estate, to the coffers of the magical academy he'd never finished or formally left; and his friends, to the chaos of Faerûn during his absence.

With all this loss and nothing but mental anguish, he turned to Shar, as all those who suffer broken minds do, and gave himself to the Dark Lady in hopes of at least one thing: respite. With the last of his father's coin drained by the academy, they commissioned an up-and-coming squire and her charge, Sophilia and Luna, to escort Adellar back home to Daggerford. The trio joined a caravan with a half-orc guard and an oddly large dog, who went by the names Rothsgyar and Sibunna. Upon reaching Daggerford, the broken 20-year-old Adellar was turned over to the care of Drayson, leader of the strange but seemingly benevolent monastery called the Temple of the Old Ones. There Adellar has lived out his days, as the town fool — or kook — until present day.

The Chronicle of Sophilia

The Nightwatch's file on Sophilia Spellmason begins later than most — not with her birth, but with a commission. When House Shadowdusk's broken heir, Adellar Varrn, had nothing left but grief and an empty purse, it was Sophilia — an up-and-coming squire at the time — and her charge Luna who were paid the last of his father's coin to see him safely home to Daggerford, alongside a half-orc guard named Rothsgyar and an oddly large dog called Sibunna.

What isn't recorded anywhere is what that journey cost her, or what she saw in a broken twenty-year-old that convinced a Mystic Fire Knight to keep watching over him long after the job was finished and paid. Sophilia serves Mystra with a paladin's rigid oaths and an aasimar's inherited grace, and underneath both, rarely shown, a stubborn refusal to let failures — hers or anyone else's — go unanswered.

Whatever oath first bound her to that escort, it hasn't loosened since. The Nightwatch has a simpler way of putting it: still on duty.
